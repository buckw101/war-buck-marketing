# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local service-business owners — lawn care, landscaping, roofing, HVAC,
contractors, tree service, auto/car detailing, pressure washing, and similar
trades. They're time-poor, skeptical of marketing agencies (often burned by
vague promises before), and evaluating War-Buck fast — often from a phone, in
a spare five minutes between jobs. They're not marketers themselves; they
want proof of results stated plainly, not jargon.

## Product Purpose

War-Buck Marketing is a marketing agency that gets local service businesses
consistent, paying customers through paid ads and organic social media (plus
website builds). The site's job is to convert a skeptical trade-business
owner into a booked call. Success looks like: the visitor immediately
understands "this agency gets results for businesses like mine" and books a
free strategy call.

## Brand Personality

Bold, premium, confident — a serious operator, not a scrappy freelancer and
not a faceless corporate SaaS vendor. Three words: **direct, high-trust,
built-different**. Emotional goal: "these people know what they're doing and
aren't wasting my time."

## Anti-references

Explicitly NOT generic pastel-blue corporate SaaS (light blue/white
gradients, rounded icon-in-box cards, uppercase tracked kickers on every
section, soft pastel badges) — that register was deliberately replaced
site-wide on 2026-07-02 because it reads as generic B2B software, not a
results-driven agency serving local trades. Any new asset (including social
share images) should carry the same near-black/bold-blue premium system, not
regress to the old look.

## Design Principles

- Prove results before making promises — lean on concrete numbers over adjectives.
- Speak in plain, direct language a busy trade-business owner reads in seconds, not marketing jargon.
- One bold saturated blue used decisively, not diluted into pastel tints.
- Confident restraint: near-black canvas + one accent color, not a full rainbow palette.
- Every asset (site pages, social cards, ads) shares one visual system — no one-off styles.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target specified; follow standard contrast best practice
(≥4.5:1 body text, ≥3:1 large text) since the design system already uses
near-black ink on light paper and light text on near-black, both of which
comfortably clear those ratios.
